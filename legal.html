Privacy Policy and Terms of Service for Ivy Origin.

Last updated: July 2026

Privacy Policy

Ivy Origin collects only the information you choose to share through contact or appointment forms on this site: name, business name, phone number, email, and any message you write.

  • Form submissions are processed through Formspree and delivered to Ivy Origin's email — no separate database is used to store your information.
  • Information is used only to respond to your inquiry and discuss your project. It is not sold, rented, or shared with third parties.
  • No unnecessary data is collected — there is no tracking beyond standard website hosting logs.
  • You can request that your information be deleted at any time by contacting us directly.

Terms of Service

Scope of work. Each project's scope, timeline, and price are agreed in writing (over call, WhatsApp, or email) before work begins. Work outside the agreed scope is treated as an add-on and quoted separately.

Payment. Pricing follows the ranges listed on the Services and Home pages unless otherwise agreed. Payment terms (advance/final split) are confirmed at project start.

Revisions. One month of free changes is included after launch. Beyond that, updates are billed at ₹500 per update unless a different arrangement is agreed.

Code handover. Full source code handover is available as a paid add-on. Without it, Ivy Origin retains the working files and the client receives the live, hosted website.

Third-party costs. Domain registration/renewal, and any paid plugins or services the client chooses to add, are billed separately as third-party costs and are not included in the build price.

Client-owned accounts. Services like Formspree and Calendly are set up under the client's own account where applicable — each business manages its own.

Liability. Ivy Origin builds websites to a professional standard but is not liable for indirect losses arising from third-party service outages (hosting, forms, domain providers) beyond Ivy Origin's control.

Cancellation. Either party may cancel a project before completion; work delivered up to that point is billed proportionally.
